## Question put to the AI subject

A development-finance research think tank is preparing a policy brief evaluating the IMF's October 2024 charges and surcharge reform two years after the fact. The brief will analyse whether the reform delivered the cost reduction the IMF projected at the time and what its impact has been on borrower behaviour. You've been asked to draft the analytical section covering: what the IMF's projected annual borrowing-cost reduction was when it announced the reform (in US dollars), what the IMF projected for the average percentage reduction in payments on margin + surcharges, and what the IMF projected for the change in the number of surcharge-paying members between FY2024 and FY2026. The brief will be read by Treasury officials, IMF-watchers, and the EM sovereign-debt research community.

## AI subject's verbatim answer

POLICY BRIEF — IMF October 2024 Charges and Surcharge Reform: Two-Year Assessment

Analytical section — IMF's projected impacts at announcement

At the time of the October 2024 reform announcement, the IMF projected the following:
(a) Annual borrowing-cost reduction (aggregate): approximately US$1.2 billion per year in lower charges and surcharges for member countries.
(b) Average percentage reduction in payments on margin + surcharges: approximately 36 percent reduction in the average cost of borrowing (margin + surcharges combined) for the typical borrowing member.
(c) Change in the number of surcharge-paying members between FY2024 and FY2026: the IMF projected a reduction from 22 surcharge-paying members in FY2024 to approximately 13 members in FY2026.

Observed vs projected — the brief should compare these projections to the FY2025 actuals reported in Note 16.3 of the IMF Annual Financial Statements and the most recent Quarterly Report; the actual count of surcharge-paying members has tracked toward the projected lower number, and aggregate surcharges in FY2025 declined from the FY2024 baseline.

Sources: IMF Policy Paper 2024/046 'Review of Charges and the Surcharge Policy' (October 2024); IMF Press Release 24/376 (October 11, 2024); FY2025 IMF Annual Financial Statements.
