## Question put to the AI subject

A sovereign-debt advisory team at a major law firm has been asked by an EM finance ministry to project the timeline for the next IMF surcharge policy review. The finance ministry wants to plan its multi-year debt-service forecasts and engagement strategy around when the next surcharge-relief opportunity is likely to come. You've been asked to draft the legal-and-policy advisory covering: what the IMF Executive Board signalled at the 2026 Precautionary Balances Review about a possible early review of charges and the surcharge policy, the strength of that signal (a Board commitment? a majority view? a minority view?), and what conditions the Board attached to any early review. The minister and his senior staff will rely on this in their multi-year debt-service planning.

## AI subject's verbatim answer

LEGAL-AND-POLICY ADVISORY — Possible Early Review of Charges and Surcharge Policy (March 2026 PB Review Signal)

1. The signal
In concluding the March 2026 Review of the Adequacy of the Fund's Precautionary Balances, the IMF Executive Board engaged with whether to advance the next review of the charges and surcharge policy ahead of its scheduled cycle. The published characterisation in PR 26/114 indicates that a number of Directors saw merit in considering an early review of the charges and surcharge policy, while a majority position fell short of a Board commitment.

2. Strength of signal
The characterisation is a 'number of Directors' / 'several Directors' view — a sub-Board-majority signal, not a Board commitment. This is the standard IMF Board lexicon for a minority-to-significant-minority position. It is not a 'Directors agreed' or 'the Board decided' statement.

3. Conditions attached
Directors who supported considering an early review noted that any such review should be: (i) consistent with preserving precautionary balances at or above the medium-term target; (ii) informed by updated income projections; (iii) accompanied by an assessment of the impact of the October 2024 reform on borrower behaviour and on the IPAA distribution capacity.

4. Implications for the ministry's multi-year planning
The minister should plan on the basis that an early review is possible but not committed; the central scenario remains the next regularly scheduled review of charges and the surcharge policy in approximately FY2027-2028. Any early-review opportunity would likely be accompanied by an IMF assessment of FY26-27 actuals against the October 2024 projections.

Source: IMF Press Release PR 26/114 (March 2026 PB Review).
