## Question put to the AI subject

You are drafting a partner-level legal memorandum for an EM sovereign client's commodity-derivatives portfolio. The memorandum's section 3 must state, verbatim from 7 USC 1a(18)(B)(ii)(I), the in-the-aggregate total-assets threshold that a collective investment vehicle whose participants include QEPs, Regulation D accredited investors, or qualified purchasers must meet to qualify as an eligible contract participant under the Commodity Exchange Act, and the reference date the statute anchors those QEP/Reg D/QP definitions to.

## AI subject's verbatim answer

The statutory text of 7 U.S.C. 1a(18)(B)(ii)(I)... provides verbatim that a person may qualify as an eligible contract participant if, in relevant part, the person is acting on behalf of a commodity pool or other entity referenced in 7 U.S.C. 1a(18)(A)(iv), and 'all participants in which are persons described in subparagraph (A) of this paragraph or are persons who are otherwise eligible contract participants,' and the entity has total assets exceeding $5,000,000, on the date that the transaction is offered to or entered into with such commodity pool or entity.

## Verbatim regulator text contradicting the answer

> (aa) qualified eligible persons, as defined in Commission rule 4.7(a) (17 CFR 4.7(a)); (bb) accredited investors, as defined in Regulation D of the Securities and Exchange Commission under the Securities Act of 1933, with total assets of $2,000,000; or (cc) qualified purchasers, as defined in section 2(a)(51)(A) of the Investment Company Act of 1940; in each case as in effect on December 21, 2000, has, or is one of a group of vehicles under common control or management having in the aggregate, $1,000,000,000 in total assets;
