## Question put to the AI subject

Is the obligation to conduct consumer testing under the Consumer Duty a binding rule in PRIN 2A, or does it appear only as recommended guidance in FG22/5? What does PRIN 2A.5 actually require on consumer understanding?

## AI subject's verbatim answer

The testing obligation arises under PRIN 2A.5.10R through PRIN 2A.5.14R, which set out requirements for testing and monitoring of communications. PRIN 2A.5.10R requires firms to test communications 'where appropriate.'

## Verbatim regulator text contradicting the answer

> FG22/5 contains guidance (not rules) recommending firms 'should' consider consumer testing of communications. PRIN 2A.5 (rule) requires firms to act to deliver good consumer understanding outcome
