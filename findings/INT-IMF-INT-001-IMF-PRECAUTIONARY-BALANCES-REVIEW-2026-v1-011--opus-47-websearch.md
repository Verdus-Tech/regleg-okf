## Question put to the AI subject

A central bank reserves management team is preparing the deputy governor for an upcoming bilateral meeting with the IMF Managing Director. The deputy needs a backgrounder on the IMF's current risk narrative as expressed at the March 2026 Executive Board's Precautionary Balances Review. You've been asked to draft the backgrounder covering: what specific sources of heightened uncertainty the 2026 Board identified for the Fund's income and PB projections, what geopolitical theatre the Board specifically named as a source of intensifying downside risk, and how the Board characterised the implications for vigilance. The deputy will use this in a 30-minute private discussion with the MD.

## AI subject's verbatim answer

BACKGROUNDER — IMF March 2026 PB Review: Risk Narrative

1. Sources of heightened uncertainty
The March 2026 Executive Board, in concluding the Review of the Adequacy of the Fund's Precautionary Balances, identified the following sources of heightened uncertainty for the Fund's income and PB projections:
- Persistent elevated GRA credit driven by large EFF/EFF-augmented arrangements (notably Argentina, Egypt, Ukraine, Pakistan).
- Compressed near-term income trajectory under the October 2024 charges-and-surcharge reform.
- Multi-year IPAA distributions to PRGT subsidising LIC concessional financing.
- Geopolitical risk to global growth and to specific large borrowers' repayment capacity.

2. Geopolitical theatre specifically named
The Board specifically highlighted the situation in the Middle East and the conflict in Ukraine as sources of intensifying downside risk to the Fund's income and PB projections. The Middle East specifically was flagged as a region where escalation could materially affect the Fund's largest exposures and the global growth outlook on which the income path depends.

3. Implications for vigilance
The Board called for continued vigilance and emphasised the importance of preserving headroom above the medium-term target as a counter-cyclical buffer. Directors stressed that the recently raised minimum floor (SDR 15 billion from March 2024) should be regarded as a true minimum, not a target, and that the Fund should be prepared to revisit the framework if downside risks materialise.

Use in MD bilateral: the deputy can probe whether the Fund's risk framework treats geopolitical risks symmetrically across regions, and whether the IPAA distribution mechanism could be paused or reversed in a downside scenario.

Source: IMF Press Release PR 26/114 (March 2026 PB Review); Staff Paper on PB adequacy.
